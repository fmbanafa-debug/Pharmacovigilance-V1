The 12 PV Practice Pillars

Essential requirements for Pharmacovigilance compliance in the MENA region, focused on SFDA GVP standards.

1. Case Processing & Signal Detection (4 Requirements)

Focuses on the timely collection, processing, and proactive analysis of safety data from various sources.

1. ICSR Timeliness

All local Individual Case Safety Reports (ICSRS) are entered into the PV system within **SFDA-defined timelines**.

2. ADR Follow-up

Follow-ups for incomplete ADR reports are performed and documented according to **SFDA timelines**.

3. Signal Review

Signal detection and review activities are conducted at the **frequency required** by SFDA GVP or internal SOPs.

4. Literature Screening

Local scientific literature is screened for potential adverse events as required by **SFDA GVP**.

2. Risk Management & Communication (3 Requirements)

Details the required actions for assessing, documenting, and communicating established and emerging risks.

5. RMP Maintenance

When new safety information arises, the local **Risk Management Plan (RMP) is updated** and submitted to SFDA as required.

6. RMM Execution

Direct Healthcare Professional Communications (DHPCs), patient cards, and other **RMMs are distributed and documented** according to the SFDA-approved plan.

7. Safety Escalation

New or emerging safety information is **escalated to SFDA and company management** through a documented procedure.

3. System & Quality Compliance (3 Requirements)

Covers the mandatory documentation and maintenance of the PV system's quality standards for auditing.

8. PSMF/PSSF Readiness

A local **PSMF / PSSF is maintained, complete, and kept up to date** for SFDA inspection.

9. SOP Recertification

PV procedures (SOPs / WIs) are **reviewed and updated regularly** to ensure continued SFDA compliance.

10. CAPA Implementation

Corrective and Preventive Actions (**CAPAs**) from SFDA inspections or audits are **implemented and closed within the agreed timelines**.

4. Oversight & Personnel (2 Requirements)

Ensuring all personnel are qualified and external partners are properly monitored.

11. Third-Party Control

PV activities performed by distributors or third-party providers are **overseen through audits, metrics, or compliance reviews**.

12. Employee Training

New employees receive **PV induction training within 30 days of joining**, as required by SFDA GVP.

Reference Document based on Pharmacovigilance Professional Survey (Practice Section).

Acronyms: SFDA (Saudi Food and Drug Authority), GVP (Good Pharmacovigilance Practices), ICSR (Individual Case Safety Report), RMP (Risk Management Plan), RMM (Risk-Minimization Measure), PSMF (PV System Master File), CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Action).
